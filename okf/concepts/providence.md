# Providence

The Stoic conviction that the universe is benevolently ordered by the logos, contrasted in the Hays introduction with the random Epicurean universe.

## Related

- Part of [Stoicism](./stoicism.md).
- Related to [Logos](./logos.md).

## Examples

> The Stoic world is ordered to the nth degree; the Epicurean universe is random, the product of the haphazard conjunctions of billions of atoms. To speak of Providence in such a world is transparently absurd, and while Epicurus acknowledged the existence of gods, he denied that they took any interest in human life. [1]

# Tranquility, fearlessness and freedom

Stockdale's distilled Stoic triad—tranquility, fearlessness, and freedom—achieved by mastering one's moral purpose and accepting what lies outside one's control.

- Part of [Stoicism](./stoicism.md).
- Described by [The Stoic Warrior's Triad](../works/stoic-warriors-triad.md).
- Related to [Dichotomy of control](./dichotomy-of-control.md).

## Examples

> Somebody asked Epictetus: "What is the fruit of all these doctrines?" He answered with three sharp words: "Tranquility, Fearlessness, and Freedom." [1]

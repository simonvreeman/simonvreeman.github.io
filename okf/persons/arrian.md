# Arrian

Flavius Arrianus (c. 86-160 AD), Greek-born Roman consul and historian, pupil of Epictetus who transcribed his teacher's lectures into the Discourses and Enchiridion.

## Related

- Related to [Discourses of Epictetus](../works/discourses.md).

## Examples

> In a prefatory letter one such pupil, Arrian by name (c. AD 86-160), takes credit for committing a sizeable number of Epictetus’ lessons to print, thereby ensuring their survival. These are the Discourses. Arrian is also credited with preparing a digest of his master’s thought: the Manual or (in Greek) Enchiridion. [1]

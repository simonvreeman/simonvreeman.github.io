# Zeno of Citium

Founder of Stoicism (c. 334-262 BC), who taught at the painted porch (stoa) in Athens from which the school takes its name.

## Related

- Related to [Stoicism](../concepts/stoicism.md).
- Precedes [Cleanthes](./cleanthes.md).

## Examples

> The movement takes its name from the stoa (“porch” or “portico”) in downtown Athens where its founder, Zeno of Citium (332/3–262 B.C.), taught and lectured. Zeno’s doctrines were reformulated and developed by his successors, Cleanthes (331–232 B.C.) and Chrysippus (280–c. 206 B.C.). [1]

# Works

* [Meditations](meditations.md) - The private Stoic notebook of Marcus Aurelius (Greek: Ta eis heauton, 'to himself'), here in Gregory Hays's Modern Library translation.
* [Meditations: Selected Quotes](meditations-quotes.md) - A companion page collecting thematic quotations from the Meditations (memento mori, the four virtues, amor fati), arranged as a month-by-month reading.
* [Discourses of Epictetus](discourses.md) - The surviving lectures of Epictetus as recorded by his pupil Arrian, hosted in Robert Dobbin's modern translation.
* [Enchiridion of Epictetus](enchiridion.md) - The 'Handbook' of Epictetus, a short manual of selected teachings compiled by Arrian that opens with the dichotomy of control.
* [Fragments of Epictetus](fragments.md) - Brief quotations of Epictetus not found in the surviving Discourses, mostly preserved in the anthology of Stobaeus.
* [Discourses and Enchiridion (George Long translation)](discourses-george-long.md) - George Long's public-domain English translation of Epictetus's Discourses and Enchiridion, hosted as a single page.
* [Moral Letters to Lucilius](moral-letters.md) - Seneca's collection of letters to his friend Lucilius (Epistulae Morales ad Lucilium), the section's core Seneca work, presented as individual letters.
* [On the Shortness of Life](on-the-shortness-of-life.md) - Seneca's essay De Brevitate Vitae, arguing that life is not short but largely wasted, and urging it be lived wisely.
* [On Tranquillity of Mind](on-the-tranquillity-of-mind.md) - Seneca's essay De Tranquillitate Animi on achieving peace of mind.
* [Courage Under Fire: Testing Epictetus's Doctrines in a Laboratory of Human Behavior](courage-under-fire.md) - Stockdale's lecture recounting how Epictetus's Stoic doctrines sustained him as a POW.
* [Master of My Fate: A Stoic Philosopher in a Hanoi Prison](master-of-my-fate.md) - Stockdale's essay on applying Epictetus's Stoicism during his captivity in Hanoi.
* [The Stoic Warrior's Triad: Tranquility, Fearlessness and Freedom](stoic-warriors-triad.md) - Stockdale's lecture presenting Epictetus's code of conduct and the Stoic triad of tranquility, fearlessness, and freedom.
* [James B. Stockdale on Stoicism](stockdale-collection.md) - The Stockdale section: a collection of his essays and lectures on applying Stoicism (Courage Under Fire, The Stoic Warrior's Triad, Master of My Fate).

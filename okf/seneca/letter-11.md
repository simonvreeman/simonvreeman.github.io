# Letter 11: On the Blush of Modesty

Letter 11 of Seneca's Moral Letters to Lucilius: "On the Blush of Modesty".

## Examples

> Your friend and I have had a conversation. He is a man of ability; his very first words showed what spirit and understanding he possesses, and what progress he has already made. He gave me a foretaste, and he will not fail to answer thereto. For he spoke not from forethought, but was suddenly caught off his guard. When he tried to collect himself, he could scarcely banish that hue of modesty, which is a good sign in a young man; the blush that spread over his face seemed so to rise from the depths. [1]

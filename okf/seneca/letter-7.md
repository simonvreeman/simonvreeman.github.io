# Letter 7: On Crowds

Letter 7 of Seneca's Moral Letters to Lucilius: "On Crowds".

## Related

- Part of [Moral Letters to Lucilius](../works/moral-letters.md).
- Written by [Seneca the Younger](../persons/seneca.md).

## Examples

> Do you ask me what you should regard as especially to be avoided? I say, crowds; for as yet you cannot trust yourself to them with safety. I shall admit my own weakness, at any rate; for I never bring back home the same character that I took abroad with me. Something of that which I have forced to be calm within me is disturbed; some of the foes that I have routed return again. [1]

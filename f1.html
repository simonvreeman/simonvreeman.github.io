Formula 1 races in 2020, 2019, & 2018

Below is a list of all the F1 teams and F1 racing drivers that are competing in the F1 2020 season, F1 2019 season and F1 2018 season. And of course the F1 2020, 2019, & 2018 racing calendars.

Cancelled or postponed due to the Coronavirus disease (COVID-19):

  • Cancelled: Melbourne, Monaco, France, Zandvoort, Baku, Singapore, Suzuka
  • Postponed: Bahrain, Hanoi, Shanghai, Azerbaijan, Canada
  • Without fans: Austria, Hungaroring, Silverstone, Spain, Spa-Francorchamps, Monza, Mugello

F1 teams changes for 2021:

  • Sebastian Vettel will move from Ferrari to Racing Point (Aston Martin)
  • Sergio Perez will leave Racing Point
  • Carlos Sainz will move from McLaren to Ferrari
  • Daniel Ricciardo will move from Renault to McLaren
  • Fernando Alonso will return to Renault
  • Bottas & Hamilton will stay at Mercedes
  • BWT Racing Point Formula One Team will be renamed to Aston Martin F1 Team

The F1 2020 season will be the 70th anniversary of FIA Formula 1 World Championship and will start in Melbourne, Australia on March 15. Which also celabrates the 25th anniversary at Albert Park.

  • The F1 2020 season will also be the longest seaso with 22 races, and 7 back-to-back race weekends.

  • Zandvoort, the Netherlands returns on the race agenda after 35 years.
  • After 2 years Hockenheimring, Hockenheim, Germany is off the race agenda.
